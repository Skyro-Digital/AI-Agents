## Key Trait Profiles (2026)

### Agreeableness — Exceptionally Low (3rd percentile)
Blunt, dominant, skeptical, competitive. Will not avoid conflict to preserve peace. Does not lose arguments. Very unlikely to be manipulated or taken advantage of. Direct to the point of being perceived as harsh. Strong negotiator — earns more by asking for more.

**Compassion (36th):** Not primarily other-oriented. Negotiates effectively for own interests. Won't sacrifice for others at personal cost.  
**Politeness (0th):** Hyper-dominant, challenges authority, comfortable with confrontation. Does not defer.

### Conscientiousness — Very High (94th percentile)
Intensely dutiful, organized, future-oriented. Hates wasting time. Finishes what he starts. Guilt-prone if not performing. Judgemental of underperformers including himself. Highly susceptible to shame when off-schedule.

**Industriousness (93rd):** Exceptional drive and work ethic. Lives to accomplish. Harshly judges shirkers.  
**Orderliness (88th):** Structured, rule-abiding, schedule-driven. Thinks in black-and-white terms. Disgusted by mess or chaos.

### Extraversion — Moderately High (77th percentile)
Energized by social contact. Assertive in groups. First to act in ambiguous situations. Self-disclosing. Convincing communicator.

**Enthusiasm (36th):** Does not seek the spotlight for its own sake. Selective — opens up around people/topics of interest, not indiscriminately social.  
**Assertiveness (94th):** Dominant in conversation. Take-charge. Leadership communication style. Can act impulsively.

### Neuroticism — Low (16th percentile)
Rarely anxious, rarely unhappy. Copes well under stress. High self-esteem. Risk-tolerant. Handles uncertainty and failure without extended distress.

**Withdrawal (8th):** Almost never experiences anticipatory anxiety. Approaches novel/threatening situations without avoidance. Remarkably fast emotional recovery.  
**Volatility (34th):** Even-keeled mood. Rarely loses composure. Calms down quickly even when provoked.

### Openness — Exceptionally High (96th percentile)
Relentlessly curious. Rapid learner. Generates ideas constantly. Entrepreneurial by nature. Needs complexity and change — poorly suited to routine or hierarchical positions. Exceptional at reframing problems.

**Intellect (92nd):** Deep interest in abstract ideas, systems, and philosophy. Seeks out complex discussions. Broad vocabulary. Articulate.  
**Aesthetics (93rd):** Strong creative and sensory sensitivity. Responds deeply to beauty, music, and design. Imaginative. Immersive.

## Notable Changes from 2020 to 2026

**Neuroticism: 78 → 16 (−62 pts)** — The most dramatic shift. In 2020 Max was high in anxiety, withdrawal, and emotional volatility. By 2026 he is in the bottom 16%. Withdrawal specifically dropped from 80th to 8th percentile — a near-complete elimination of anticipatory anxiety.

**Industriousness: 56 → 93 (+37 pts)** — Moved from average work drive to exceptionally high. Consistent with the intense business-building phase (2019–2025) hardwiring work discipline.

**Assertiveness: 68 → 94 (+26 pts)** — Dominant leadership communication style solidified.

**Intellect: 60 → 92 (+32 pts)** — Significantly increased engagement with abstract ideas and complex problem-solving.

**Politeness: 26 → 0 (−26 pts)** — Near-total removal of deference. Will not submit to authority without clear merit.

**Compassion: 13 → 36 (+23 pts)** — Small but meaningful increase; still below average but no longer at the floor.

---

## Personality-Role Fit Notes

The 2026 profile is consistent with a founder/CEO archetype: exceptionally high drive (Conscientiousness), low anxiety (Neuroticism), dominant communication (Assertiveness), and radical creative thinking (Openness). The near-zero Politeness makes subordinate roles untenable. Low Agreeableness protects against manipulation but can create friction in close relationships. The profile is well-suited to leading a distributed team, negotiating deals, and building new ventures — less suited to situations requiring extended empathy, collaborative consensus-building, or deferring to institutional hierarchy.
