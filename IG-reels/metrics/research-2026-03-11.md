## TRENDING TOPICS (March 2026)

### 1. Gmail Gemini AI Inbox — Email's Biggest Metric Distortion

**What happened:** Google announced Gmail's "Gemini era" on January 8, 2026 — rolling out AI-powered inbox summaries, priority sorting, and thread summarization powered by Gemini 3, on by default.

**Impact on metrics (per third-party deliverability researchers):**
- Open rates rising to ~45%+ (analysts believe Gmail pre-fetches email content to generate summaries, firing the open pixel before users read)
- CTR declining as subscribers read the AI summary without opening the full email
- Deliverability researchers estimate up to 40% of emails are being deprioritized by Gmail's content quality filter
- First ~150 characters now heavily weighted in what the AI displays
- Gmail evaluates content clarity and structure as a quality signal

**Note on sourcing:** The open rate/CTR shift data comes from third-party deliverability tool analysis (Folderly, Backstroke), not official Google disclosure. Frame as "analysts believe" rather than confirmed fact.

**Why it matters for Max's audience:** Most DTC operators are misreading their data — open rates look healthy but click rates are declining and they don't know why.

Sources: Folderly.com, emailexpert.com, Klaviyo Blog on Gmail Gemini, CNBC (Jan 8, 2026)

### 3. Bot Clicks Corrupting Email Performance Data

**Confirmed data:**
- Estimates put bot clicks at 20-60% of all email clicks (Paved research cites ~20% as a baseline; rates can reach 40%+ — range varies by list type)
- Corporate email security systems (Proofpoint, Mimecast) automatically click every link before delivery as a security scan
- This corrupts A/B tests, distorts flow performance, and causes false unsubscribe rate inflation
- Brands whose flows branch on "clicked email 1" may be giving discounts to security bots, not customers

**Why it matters:** Max's audience is making flow branching decisions based on click signals that are significantly corrupted. First creator in the niche to cover this in DTC terms.

Sources: Validity, Mailjet, Cyberimpact, Paved research

### 6. Identity Resolution / Wunderkind + Klaviyo

**What happened:** Wunderkind joined the Klaviyo Marketplace on January 28, 2026 (confirmed: BusinessWire, January 28, 2026).

**What it does:** Identifies anonymous website visitors using first-party data and Wunderkind's identity network (1B+ profiles), matching them to known email addresses without cookie tracking.

**Official claims (from Wunderkind's Klaviyo Marketplace launch press release):**
- Brands using Wunderkind have seen "up to 8x increases in triggered revenue"
- One case study brand de-anonymized 35% of site traffic
- Note: Wunderkind claims to identify "up to 95% of anonymous visitors" from profiles already in a brand's CRM or their network — the identification rate depends heavily on CRM size and traffic source mix

**Legal note:** Wunderkind describes itself as GDPR/CCPA compliant and first-party/cookie-free — but the "fully legal" characterization is contested; frame as "privacy-compliant by design."

**Why it matters:** Typical popup opt-in: 2-5% of traffic. This can identify a much larger portion of anonymous traffic for triggered flows.

Sources: BusinessWire January 28, 2026, Wunderkind official website
