### 2. Gmail Hard Rejection Enforcement (November 2025) — Still Highly Relevant
Starting November 2025, Gmail moved from educational warnings to actively rejecting non-compliant emails outright. Not spam-filtering — permanent 5xx bounce rejection. This affects any brand sending bulk email without proper DMARC alignment.

**Key Facts:**
- Bulk senders exceeding 0.3% spam complaint threshold face immediate rejection
- Google retired Domain Reputation and IP Reputation dashboards from Postmaster Tools in September 2025 — the metrics brands relied on are gone
- SPF, DKIM, DMARC now bare minimum; Gmail enforces all three
- Brands failing "Compliance Status" check in Postmaster Tools v2 get rejected before reaching spam

**Why It's Still Trending:** Most DTC brands haven't checked their Postmaster Tools since the dashboard changed. Many are sending to compromised deliverability without knowing it.

### 6. Email Deliverability as a Crisis Topic (2026)
Brands that actively manage list hygiene achieve inbox placement rates 8-12% higher than those that don't. The core problem: most DTC brands treat deliverability as a technical checkbox, not an ongoing strategy.

**Current Deliverability Threats:**
- Gmail's engagement-based filtering: low CTOR = promotional tab demotion = further engagement decline (death spiral)
- Spam complaint threshold: 0.3% triggers Gmail rejection; most brands don't monitor this in real time
- Postmaster Tools dashboard changes: reputation metrics are gone, replaced by compliance status
- Apple MPP (Mail Privacy Protection): inflated open rates masking real engagement collapse
- Suppression management: most brands aren't sunsetting unengaged contacts aggressively enough

**Key Stats:**
- Segmented emails see 30% more opens and 50% more click-throughs (B2B, directionally applies to DTC)
- Brands achieving 80% fewer bounces through active suppression see 30% increase in opens
- Recovering sender reputation after Gmail issues takes 4-8 weeks minimum

### 9. Email Open Rates Are Lying to You (Apple MPP + Engagement Collapse)
Apple Mail Privacy Protection preloads tracking pixels whether or not a subscriber reads the email — artificially inflating reported open rates. Combined with Apple Mail's Digest View (covered in Reel 3), brands may think their list is healthy when it isn't.

**What to Measure Instead:**
- Click-to-Open Rate (CTOR) — real engagement indicator
- Revenue per recipient — ties engagement directly to business outcome
- Unsubscribe rate — leading indicator of deliverability problems
- Placed order rate from email — the only metric that matters for DTC

**Key Fact:** Ecommerce average email campaign open rate is 37.93% (inflated by MPP). Top-performing campaigns see 54.78%. Welcome emails average 83.63%. These numbers are misleading without CTOR context.
