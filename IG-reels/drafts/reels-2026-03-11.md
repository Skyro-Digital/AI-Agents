## Reel 2: Bot Clicks Are Giving Discounts to Robots

**Why Now**: Bot clicks are estimated at 20-60% of all email clicks and are silently corrupting flow logic — nobody in the DTC creator space is talking about this.

**Format**: Screen recording walkthrough

**Visual Notes**: Show a Klaviyo flow builder with a conditional split branching on "clicked email 1." Zoom in on the branch logic. Then show an alternative — branching on "placed order" or "viewed product page" instead. Show a Klaviyo analytics dashboard with an inflated click number highlighted.

**Headlines**:
> 1. "Your Discount Flows Are Sending To BOTS Right Now"
> 2. "20-60% Of Your Email Clicks Are FAKE. Fix This"
> 3. "Bot Clicks Are BREAKING Your Flow Logic. Here's Proof"
> 4. "Your A/B Tests Are WRONG Because Of This"
> 5. "BOTS Are Getting Your Best Discounts. Not Customers"

**Hook**:
> If your abandoned cart flow gives a discount to anyone who clicked email one — you're giving discounts to bots. Not customers. Bots.

**Script**:
> Here's what's happening.

> Corporate email security systems — Proofpoint, Mimecast — click every link in every email before the employee sees it. Automatically. It's a security scan. The human never sees the email first — the bot does.

> Research estimates bot clicks at 20 to 60% of all email clicks depending on your list type.

> So when your flow branches here — [SHOW: Klaviyo flow conditional split on "clicked email 1"] — and sends the discount to everyone who "clicked"... a chunk of that is bots triggering the discount. Not real buyers.

> It also corrupts your A/B tests. If you're testing which version gets more clicks, your winner might just be whichever version the bots preferred.

> The fix is straightforward. [SHOW: New branch logic based on "placed order" or "viewed product"] Branch on purchase events. Branch on site visits. Not email clicks.

> Clicks as a signal are broken. Start using downstream behavior — did they buy, did they visit the site — as your branching logic instead.

> This one change will immediately clean up your flow performance data.

**On-Screen Text/Caption**:
> Bot clicks = 20-60% of all clicks / Don't branch on clicks → branch on purchases or site visits / Fix this in your flow builder today

**Practical Takeaway**:
> Go into your abandoned cart and post-purchase flows. Anywhere you branch on "clicked email X," switch it to "placed order" or "viewed product page."
